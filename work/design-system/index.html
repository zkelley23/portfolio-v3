Vasion Design System

Overview

What ultimately ended up as the Vasion Design System started as a branding project. The UX team was working on a product in the early-development stage when my manager asked me to brainstorm ways to improve the branding in the product. At the time, the product was very stark and bland. It also lacked character and branding. I started thinking of ways to pull more of our brand logos and colors into the interface.

While this was going on, it also became clear that we, as a UX team, needed a better design system. We had been using one that was only partially complete, which didn't fit our needs. One of my teammates and I did some research to find out the best way forward.

Research

We researched design system creation and maintenance and noted best practices. We studied popular design systems to better understand what we wanted to implement at Vasion. My teammate suggested using the Material Design 3 component library since it was available on Figma. We started poking around the Figma file provided by Material Design, and we determined that it would be perfect for the team.

Process

The component library provided by Material Design was perfect because it had everything we needed prebuilt. However, the design language had to be updated to match our vision and product. I updated things like border radiuses, font cases, and drop shadows. I worked with my teammate to update the colors to match our needs while maintaining Material Design's color theory. I also added several custom components to help speed up the design process for the team. As things went along, the Vasion Design System started coming together.

As a team, we met to discuss the system's progress and ensure alignment on component usage and design language. Even now that the design system is finished, we continue to hold weekly design language meetings to talk about components and their usage across our current designs.

Lessons Learned

It was an amazing experience working on this project. I learned so much about design systems. I learned how to build, update, and maintain a robust design system. I also found ways to improve how components and their variants are built to speed up the design process for the designers. I also learned the importance of ongoing collaboration for our design language. Thanks to the Vasion Design System, UX team is now functioning more efficiently and cohesively.
