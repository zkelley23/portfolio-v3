Download History

Overview

At the BYU Library, a patron's checkout history is saved. The Library decided that they needed to stop keeping a complete history for each patron, so they chose to purge each patron's data that was more than five years old. They wanted to give the option to the patrons to download their history before it got deleted, and I was asked to find a solution.

Research

I researched how date pickers are used on other websites. I looked at how they function and what they look like. I wanted to find a date picker style that was common enough to be easy for users to use, but that could also be used for the specific need to download Library history. As I researched, I read a few articles on best practices for date selection components. I found an example of a date picker that I thought fit the criteria that I mentioned earlier.

Process

With the findings of my research in mind, I started to brainstorm different ideas. I started sketching out these ideas and I came up with a few different sketches. The sketches demonstrated different possible workflows.

I showed my sketches to my supervisor and I received feedback from him. He and I were able to discuss the benefits and drawbacks of each of the different workflows. We picked the one we thought was the best, and from there I was able to sketch out more details relevant to the workflow we picked.

Then I started building a prototype of the sketch in Adobe XD. First I created the essential screens to get the idea across. I showed this initial prototype to the team and got their feedback. I was able to implement their feedback as I finished building the prototype. Once I finished, I showed the team again and they really liked how it turned out.

After receiving good feedback from the team, I began testing the prototype with users. Each user test ran smoothly. After several tests, it was apparent that the design was usable and that the users could easily complete the tasks I had given them.

Lessons learned

I learned a lot from this quick project. I learned how there are several different ways to achieve the same solution. I was also reminded of the importance of brainstorming and how my first idea usually isn't the best one. I studied the best practices for date selection, and I made sure to implement them. I honed my skills in Adobe XD and was able to make a dynamic prototype that was easy to edit. In the end, I was able to create a practical interface because of the things that I learned.

Zach Kelley
