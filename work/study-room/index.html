Study Room Reservation Redesign

Overview

As I was looking through the BYU Library website, I noticed that the Study Room Scheduling page could be improved. I talked with my supervisor about it, and she gave me permission to start working on a redesign since it had already been on the office's radar.

Research

I began by thinking about how students currently schedule a study room. I also thought about how students would ideally schedule a study room. The Study Room Scheduler was not mobile-friendly at all. I decided that I would focus on the mobile experience first because it would be much more convenient for a student to schedule a study room on their phone instead of their computer. I also decided to focus my initial redesign efforts on designing the feature into the BYU iOS app instead of just a mobile website version. This would make it even easier for a student to schedule a study room.

I researched how a scheduling app should work. I found inspiration in all sorts of places. I also researched and read Apple's Human Interface Guidelines for iOS. This gave me insight into how my interface should work. I also learned about using Apple's design language for the app. By designing an app using Apple's components, I would be able to design screens and interactions that the users would already be familiar with. Plus, the BYU app was already built this way so I wanted to match it.

Process

I started brainstorming and sketching out some ideas. I referenced the Human Interface Guidelines frequently so that I could match the drawings to iOS components. After different iterations of screens and user flows, I was able to solidify my ideas. Once the sketches were complete, I showed them to my supervisor. She was really excited to see my work, and she gave me feedback. I then showed the head developer and got his input as well.

←Scroll→

Next steps

The next step in this project will be conducting user research and testing. I need to talk with users to find out what is most important to them when they go to schedule a study room. I also need to interview users and show them my sketches to see if they think my idea would be useful.

As for now, the project is on hold. The redesign of this feature won't be happening for some time due to a current lack of developers in the office.

Lessons learned

This was my first project for mobile. I was able to study and learn a lot about interface and interaction design for mobile. As mentioned above, I learned a lot about Apple's Human Interface Guidelines. I learned how those guidelines are put into play when designing a mobile app. I learned how to cut out unnecessary clutter and simplify screens. This helped me design screens where the user could focus on their goal and easily complete it. I also learned a lot about the importance of wireframing and user flows.
